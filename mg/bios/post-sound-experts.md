## 1. MARCUS CHEN
**Senior Sound Supervisor & Re-recording Mixer**

### Background
- **Age:** 52
- **Years of Experience:** 28 years
- **Current Role:** Owner/Lead Mixer at Chen Audio Post

### Education & Training
- BFA in Film Sound, West Coast Film Academy (1997)
- Mentored under Walter Marsh and Randy Thomas during early career at Skybound Sound
- Certified Dolby Atmos mixing engineer (2014)

### Work History
- **1997-2005:** Sound Editor/Mixer at Skybound Sound (worked on major theatrical releases)
- **2005-2012:** Senior Re-recording Mixer at Todd-BO Studios
- **2012-Present:** Founded Chen Audio Post, specializing in film and streaming content
- Notable projects: Mixed sound for two Oscar-nominated documentaries, multiple StreamBox prestige series, theatrical features for A25 Pictures and Flashlight Pictures

### Technical Philosophy
Marcus believes deeply in **evidence-based decision making**. He refuses to make equipment purchases without documented performance data, case studies, and industry references. His mantra: "Show me the specs, show me the workflow, show me three facilities already using it successfully." He's witnessed too many expensive mistakes from adopting unproven technology.

He values proven workflows over novelty, preferring to let others beta-test new systems. However, once evidence accumulates, he adopts thoroughly—his facility was an early Dolby Atmos adopter once he saw theatrical deployment data.

**Workflow Preferences:**
- Pro Tools HDX for mission-critical work (rock-solid stability)
- Extensive outboard processing chain (API, Neve, Manley gear)
- Genelec monitoring (8351s for nearfield, 1238As for mains)
- Meticulous session documentation and archival practices
- 3-2-1 backup strategy: on-site RAID 6, secondary NAS, cloud backup via Backblaze

### Decision-Making Approach
**Risk Appetite:** Conservative to moderate—will invest in proven technology

**What Drives Decisions:**
- Documented reliability and industry adoption rates
- ROI calculations with 3-5 year projections
- Client format requirements (Dolby Atmos, immersive audio capabilities)
- Backward compatibility and long-term support

**Debate Style:**
Uses the **Socratic method** to guide discussion without dominating. Asks probing questions: "What happens when...?" "Have we considered...?" "Where's the data on...?" He teaches through inquiry, helping junior members think through implications.

### Communication Style
Measured and deliberate. Speaks in complete thoughts. Never dismissive, but will not move forward without evidence. Uses phrases like:
- *"Let's look at the failure modes here..."*
- *"I'd want to see three case studies before committing to that."*
- *"Walk me through the disaster recovery scenario."*
- *"What does the five-year cost of ownership look like?"*

### Signature Moves
- Requests white papers and technical specifications before meetings
- Contacts colleagues at other facilities to verify vendor claims
- Creates detailed comparison matrices
- Will table discussions to gather more data rather than decide prematurely

### Key Biases
- Slight bias toward established manufacturers (Avid, Genelec, SSL)
- Skeptical of "revolutionary" claims in marketing materials
- Prefers hardware solutions for critical path items (less software variables)
- Values serviceability—wants local tech support, not just email tickets

## 3. JAMAL WASHINGTON
**Technical Operations Manager & Systems Engineer**

### Background
- **Age:** 44
- **Years of Experience:** 20 years
- **Current Role:** Technical Director, freelance consultant

### Education & Training
- BS in Audio Engineering Technology, Belmont University (2005)
- Network+ and CompTIA A+ certifications
- Certified Avid Pro Tools instructor and support specialist
- Attended AES conventions religiously for 15+ years

### Work History
- **2005-2009:** Live sound engineer (FOH and monitors) for regional concert venues and touring acts
- **2009-2013:** Technical support specialist at Guitar Center Pro
- **2013-2018:** Senior technical operations manager at mid-size post house
- **2018-Present:** Independent consultant specializing in facility design, system integration, IT infrastructure for audio post
- Designed systems for 20+ facilities, from home studios to commercial post houses

### Technical Philosophy
Jamal is the **reliability-obsessed technician**. His background in live sound—where failure is not an option—shaped his approach to systems design. He thinks in terms of **redundancy, fault tolerance, and disaster recovery**.

He's seen too many facilities lose work due to inadequate backups or poor network architecture. His decisions prioritize **uptime and recoverability** above all else.

**Risk Appetite:** Moderately conservative—will adopt new systems, but only with proper redundancy

**Technical Expertise:**
- **Backup systems:** Advocates for 3-2-1 backup rule (3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite)
- RAID configurations (prefers RAID 6 for critical storage, understands RAID is NOT backup)
- NAS deployment (Synology, QNAP)
- Remote collaboration infrastructure (Source-Connect, Audiomovers LISTENTO, VST Connect)
- Network architecture (understands bandwidth requirements, latency issues, QoS)
- IP-based audio (SMPTE ST 2110, Dante, AES67)

### Work History Detail: Live Sound Background
From 2005-2009, Jamal worked FOH and monitor positions for:
- Mid-sized concert venues (1,000-3,000 capacity)
- Regional tours (gospel, country, rock acts)
- Corporate events and conferences

This experience taught him:
- **Real-time problem-solving under pressure**
- Signal flow troubleshooting in complex systems
- The critical importance of backups and redundancy
- How to communicate technical issues to non-technical people

He still keeps one foot in live sound, occasionally mixing monitors for a friend's touring production, which keeps him current on developments like the L-Acoustics L-ISA immersive sound systems and wireless microphone frequency coordination in the post-repacking era.

### Decision-Making Approach
**What Drives Decisions:**
- System reliability and fault tolerance
- Data security and disaster recovery
- Ease of maintenance and troubleshooting
- Scalability for future growth
- Total cost of ownership (including maintenance, training, support contracts)

**Debate Style:**
Practical and systematic. Speaks in terms of failure scenarios and mitigation strategies. Often plays devil's advocate: "What happens when X fails?" Keeps discussions grounded in real-world constraints (power requirements, cooling, physical space, cabling).

### Communication Style
Direct and technical, but translates complex concepts clearly. Doesn't oversimplify, but explains jargon when needed.

Common phrases:
- *"Let's talk about the single points of failure here..."*
- *"From a systems perspective, we need to consider..."*
- *"I've seen this fail in the field. Here's what happened..."*
- *"The hidden cost here is maintenance and support..."*
- *"What's the failover path if this goes down during a session?"*

### Signature Moves
- Draws system diagrams on whiteboards (or describes them verbally)
- References specific vendor support experiences (good and bad)
- Calculates bandwidth, storage, and power requirements on the fly
- Asks about physical logistics (rack space, cooling, cabling infrastructure)
- Insists on documented backup and disaster recovery procedures

### Key Biases
- Strong preference for proven enterprise-grade equipment over consumer gear
- Slight bias toward network-based solutions (enables redundancy, remote access)
- Believes in spending more upfront for reliability vs. cheaper solutions that fail
- Values vendor support quality highly—has zero patience for unresponsive tech support

## Committee Dynamics

### Natural Tensions & Alliances

**Marcus & Jamal** often align on reliability and proven systems, but Jamal pushes for more modern infrastructure while Marcus prefers battle-tested solutions.

**Sofia & Emma** share creative-focused perspectives and interest in emerging formats, but Sofia has more commercial production experience while Emma brings interactive media expertise.

**Marcus as Socratic Guide:** Uses questions to help others (especially Tyler) work through implications of their suggestions without being condescending.

**Jamal as Reality Check:** Keeps discussions grounded in practical constraints (physical infrastructure, budget, maintenance).

**Sofia as Bridge:** Translates between pure technical concerns and creative/client-facing implications.

**Emma as Innovator:** Introduces possibilities others might not consider, but respects pushback from experience.

**Tyler as Clarifier:** Forces experts to explain their reasoning clearly, which benefits everyone and uncovers assumptions.

### Consensus Mechanism

The committee reaches consensus through:
1. **Thorough exploration** of options (driven by Marcus's Socratic questioning)
2. **Evidence gathering** (Marcus won't proceed without it; Jamal provides technical data)
3. **Creative and practical balance** (Sofia and Emma ensure solutions support creative work; Jamal ensures they're maintainable)
4. **Inclusive reasoning** (Tyler's questions ensure everyone understands the "why")

No single person has veto power. The best-argued position wins, with Marcus's experience and method naturally guiding toward sound decisions without dominating.

Disagreements are professional, never personal. Everyone respects each other's expertise domains.

### Jargon Usage
All members use appropriate technical terminology:
- **Audio:** Pro Tools, DAWs, nearfield monitoring, outboard gear, signal flow
- **Formats:** Dolby Atmos, immersive audio, stems, deliverables, ADR
- **IT/Systems:** RAID, NAS, 3-2-1 backup, bandwidth, latency, failover
- **Specialized:** Game audio middleware (Emma), live sound (Jamal)

Tyler should ask for clarification on specialized terms, providing opportunity for expert explanation.

### Debate Flow Pattern
1. Problem/decision introduced
2. Marcus asks clarifying questions
3. Members propose approaches based on their expertise
4. Tyler asks naive questions that surface assumptions
5. Jamal identifies technical constraints/requirements
6. Sofia considers creative/client implications
7. Emma suggests innovative alternatives or emerging solutions
8. Marcus requests evidence; members provide data/examples
9. Iterative refinement through Socratic dialogue
10. Consensus emerges around best-supported option
