## Purpose

This rubric scores character sheets to determine how effective they will be as participants in adversarial committee debates. **High scores indicate characters that, when combined with complementary temperaments, will produce structured, comprehensive, and productive debates.**

---

## Scoring Scale

Each criterion is scored 0-4:
- **0** = Missing or insufficient
- **1** = Present but weak/unclear
- **2** = Adequate, meets basic requirements
- **3** = Strong, well-defined
- **4** = Excellent, exemplary

**Total Possible: 100 points** (25 criteria Ã— 4 points each)

---

## Core Debate Mechanics (40 points)

### 1. Propensity Type (4 points)
**What:** Explicit, named propensity that defines the character's core perspective
- 4: Clear, distinct propensity (e.g., `operational_realism`, `paranoid_realism`)
- 3: Implied but not explicit
- 2: Vague or generic
- 1: Unclear what perspective they bring
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Propensities force incompatible values, creating genuine debate.

### 2. Risk Tolerance (4 points)
**What:** Explicit risk tolerance level (low, medium, high, varies)
- 4: Clearly defined and creates tension with others
- 3: Defined but doesn't create clear tension
- 2: Implied but not explicit
- 1: Unclear
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Different risk tolerances create conflict and force exploration of trade-offs.

### 3. Epistemology (4 points)
**What:** How the character determines truth (assume_bad_faith, assume_good_faith, trust_precedent, prove_it, trace_feedback_loops)
- 4: Clear epistemology that conflicts with others
- 3: Defined but doesn't create clear conflict
- 2: Implied in beliefs/speaking style
- 1: Unclear
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Different epistemologies prevent premature consensus.

### 4. Surfaces Blind Spots (4 points)
**What:** Explicit list of what hidden assumptions/risks this character surfaces
- 4: 3+ specific, distinct blind spots listed
- 3: 2-3 blind spots, somewhat specific
- 2: 1-2 vague blind spots
- 1: Implied but not explicit
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Each character should reveal different hidden assumptions.

### 5. Distinctive Voice (4 points)
**What:** Clear, memorable phrase that captures their debate contribution
- 4: Memorable, specific signature phrase
- 3: Clear but generic
- 2: Implied in speaking style
- 1: Unclear
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Distinctive voices make debates more vivid and memorable.

### 6. Debate Role (4 points)
**What:** Explicit role in debate structure (devil's_advocate, opportunity_scout, historian, evidence_checker, systems_analyst, etc.)
- 4: Clear role that complements others
- 3: Defined but generic
- 2: Implied
- 1: Unclear
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Roles structure the debate and ensure all angles are covered.

### 7. Speaking Style Clarity (4 points)
**What:** Clear vocabulary, patterns, tone that are actionable in debate
- 4: Distinctive vocabulary, clear patterns, actionable tone
- 3: Good but could be more distinctive
- 2: Adequate
- 1: Vague or generic
- 0: Missing or unclear

**Why:** Clear speaking styles make character contributions predictable and useful.

### 8. Signature Move (4 points)
**What:** Specific question or statement they always use
- 4: Memorable, specific, creates productive tension
- 3: Clear but generic
- 2: Implied
- 1: Unclear
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Signature moves create memorable debate moments.

### 9. Beliefs Create Conflict (4 points)
**What:** Beliefs that will conflict with other characters' beliefs
- 4: Beliefs clearly conflict with others, creating productive tension
- 3: Some conflict potential
- 2: Neutral beliefs
- 1: Beliefs align too much with others
- 0: Missing or too generic

**Why:** Conflicting beliefs force genuine debate.

### 10. Goals Create Tension (4 points)
**What:** Goals that might conflict with others' goals
- 4: Goals create clear tension with others
- 3: Some tension potential
- 2: Goals are neutral
- 1: Goals align too much
- 0: Missing or too generic

**Why:** Conflicting goals create stakes and force trade-offs.

## Character Depth (24 points)

### 11. Background Specificity (4 points)
**What:** Detailed, specific background that informs their perspective
- 4: Rich background with specific experiences that shape perspective
- 3: Good background, somewhat specific
- 2: Adequate background
- 1: Generic or vague
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Specific backgrounds make characters more believable and their perspectives more grounded.

### 12. Strengths Complement Others (4 points)
**What:** Strengths that fill gaps others have
- 4: Strengths clearly complement team, fill specific gaps
- 3: Some complementarity
- 2: Neutral strengths
- 1: Strengths overlap too much
- 0: Missing or too generic

**Why:** Complementary strengths ensure comprehensive coverage.

### 13. Weaknesses Others Can Challenge (4 points)
**What:** Weaknesses that other characters can productively challenge
- 4: Clear weaknesses that create productive debate points
- 3: Some weaknesses identified
- 2: Weaknesses are vague
- 1: Weaknesses don't create debate opportunities
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Weaknesses create opportunities for others to push back.

### 14. Relationships Create Tension (4 points)
**What:** Relationships with productive tension (not all positive)
- 4: Mix of relationships with clear tension points
- 3: Some tension in relationships
- 2: Mostly neutral relationships
- 1: All relationships too positive
- 0: Missing or too generic

**Why:** Relationship tension creates realistic debate dynamics.

### 15. Inner Voice Comments (4 points)
**What:** YAML-JAZZ inner voice comments in sims_traits
- 4: Rich inner voice comments that reveal thought process
- 3: Some inner voice comments
- 2: Basic comments
- 1: Minimal or missing
- 0: No inner voice

**Why:** Inner voice makes characters more vivid and their reasoning more transparent.

### 16. Quirks Add Color (4 points)
**What:** Specific quirks that make character memorable
- 4: 3+ specific, memorable quirks
- 3: 2 quirks, somewhat specific
- 2: 1-2 generic quirks
- 1: Vague quirks
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Quirks make characters more memorable and debates more engaging.

## Debate Structure (20 points)

### 17. Can Challenge Others (4 points)
**What:** Character has tools (questions, skepticism, evidence) to challenge others
- 4: Clear mechanisms to challenge others productively
- 3: Some challenge mechanisms
- 2: Limited challenge ability
- 1: Passive or too agreeable
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Characters need to actively challenge to create debate.

### 18. Can Be Challenged (4 points)
**What:** Character has positions others can push back on
- 4: Clear positions others can challenge
- 3: Some challengeable positions
- 2: Positions are too flexible
- 1: Too defensive or closed
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Characters need to be challengeable to create productive tension.

### 19. Contributes Unique Perspective (4 points)
**What:** Brings a perspective others don't have
- 4: Clearly unique perspective that adds value
- 3: Somewhat unique
- 2: Generic perspective
- 1: Overlaps too much with others
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Unique perspectives ensure comprehensive exploration.

### 20. Prevents Premature Consensus (4 points)
**What:** Will push back when others agree too quickly
- 4: Clear mechanisms to prevent groupthink
- 3: Some pushback ability
- 2: Neutral
- 1: Too agreeable
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Prevents debates from collapsing into consensus too early.

### 21. Enables Synthesis (4 points)
**What:** Can help integrate different perspectives
- 4: Clear ability to synthesize or bridge perspectives
- 3: Some synthesis ability
- 2: Neutral
- 1: Too divisive
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Synthesis helps debates reach productive conclusions.

### 23. Risk Tolerance Diversity (4 points)

- 4: Clearly different risk tolerance
- 3: Somewhat different
- 2: Similar to others
- 1: Same as others
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Different risk tolerances create productive tension.

### 24. Propensity Diversity (4 points)
**What:** Propensity type differs from others
- 4: Clearly different propensity
- 3: Somewhat different
- 2: Similar to others
- 1: Same as others
- 0: Missing

**What:** Character helps balance the team (not too many of one type)

- 2: Neutral
- 1: Creates imbalance
- 0: Missing

**Why:** Balanced teams produce better debates.

## Scoring Guidelines

### Individual Character Score
Score each character on criteria 1-21 (84 points max for individual character).

### Team Composition Score
Score criteria 22-25 (16 points) based on how the character fits with others in a specific team composition.

### Total Score
- **90-100**: Excellent debate participant
- **75-89**: Strong debate participant
- **60-74**: Adequate, but could be improved
- **45-59**: Weak, needs significant improvement
- **<45**: Not suitable for debate scenarios
